A 5-man group project that we developed over 5 months during the fall of 2025. It is a collaborative app for gamers to find other players to play with.

Features:

The app consists of a wide variety of features, all of which we designed and built ourselves for maximum challenge. The app includes a login system via Google account or email/password. It also has a live chat that users can use to communicate one-on-one or in groups. The app includes a full notification system for messages and friend requests. Additionally, it has a friend system between users, reactive online status mechanism and a large variety of games to find players from.

Techs

Angular, JavaScript, TypeScript, WebSockets, Jest, Cypress, Google OAuth 2.0, AWS (DynamoDB, Cognito, CloudFront, Serverless Framework), Git + GitHub Actions.

My role:

I focused on the technical side of the development, mainly on frontend, but I also helped on the backend. I played a key role in planning the app's overall infrastructure and folder structure. On the frontend, I majorly worked on the overall structure, live chat, login features, routing, guards and other safety releated topics, loading animation placements, connecting backend and frontend, unit testing and smaller quality of life improvements. On the backend, I worked on some of the logics releaded to the chat and Google login, and built some lambda functions. I also built a GitHub Actions workflow that runs tests and deploys the project to AWS CloudFront. Additionally, I worked as a Scrum master, planning sprints and organizing meetings with the team.

What I learned:

During this project I learned a lot. Some of the biggest things are developing and working in a team, using Scrum, Git usage, websockets, Google OAuth, testing and overall about everything that might appear while working on a slightly larger project.
